A Brief History

Guinea pigs were first domesticated in South America around 2000bc. Originally they were kept for food but later they started being cared for as pets. They first started speaking in human languages in 260bc. Naturally, as they lived in South America, they started with Portuguese and Spanish. Today, guiea pigs can speak over 50 human languages, including English, Mandarin, Japanese, and French, although most of them only speak Spanish.
